Grid-tied: Your solar system is connected to HECO. You export excess power and draw from the grid when your panels aren't producing. No battery required, but you lose power during outages (utility safety requirement). Hybrid: Grid-connected but with battery storage. You can use your batteries during outages and reduce grid reliance. Off-grid: Completely independent from the utility. Requires careful sizing to ensure you have enough power through cloudy days and high-use periods. We design all three and help you understand which makes sense for your property.

The physical installation for a residential system typically takes 1–3 days. The bigger variable is permitting and utility interconnection, which can range from a few weeks to a couple of months depending on your county and utility situation. We'll walk you through the full timeline during your consultation so there are no surprises.

On a standard grid-tied or hybrid system, excess power is exported to the utility grid. On a fully off-grid system, the charge controller typically curtails (reduces) production once batteries hit 100%. One of our current R&D projects is a residential thermal storage system that captures that excess energy and uses it for water heating, cooking, and dehumidification — so nothing goes to waste. Stay tuned.

Puna has one of the least reliable grid segments in the state — outages are more frequent here than almost anywhere else in Hawaii.
